Brittaney Pertsas is a French Horn player that currently attends the University of Miami in Coral Gables, Florida. Originally from Tarpon Springs, Florida, she began playing the French Horn when she was in the 6th grade at Tarpon Springs Middle School. Although she got an early start, she didn't begin pursuing private lessons and auditioning for Music Programs and schools until her Junior year of High School. Brittaney currently studies with Richard Todd at the University of Miami and majors in Music Performance. In the summer of 2017, she attended the Orford Wind Octet program where she was able to study with Jeff Nelsen, Jamie Sommerville, and had coachings with various members of the Orchestra Symphonique de Montreal and the Toronto Symphony Orchestra. Brittaney also attended the Banff Chamber Music program in the summer of 2017, where she received coachings from groups including the Imani Winds and the International Contemporary Ensemble (ICE). This upcoming summer, Brittaney will be attending the Aspen Music Festival from June 19th until August 20th. During her time at the University of Miami, Brittaney has played with the Frost Wind Ensemble, Frost Symphony Orchestra, Frost Studio Jazz Band, the Henry Mancini Institute, and various chamber ensembles. Brittaney's latest accomplishments include being named a semi-finalist at the New World Symphony and getting on the sublist at the Miami City Ballet.
